Verb Formation & Common Prefixes & Suffixes

1. Prefixes for Forming Verbs

Verbs often start with be-, en-, em-, which give a verb a specific action or transformation.

Prefix Meaning Examples
be- To make or treat as befit, befool, belittle, bewail, besiege
en- To cause or become enlighten, enable, endanger, enrich, enslave
em- To put into or provide with embalm, embrace, empower, embolden, embark

2. Suffixes for Forming Verbs

Verbs often end with -ate, -en, -er, -ish, -ise (ize), -se, -fy, -it, which indicate an action or process.

Suffix Meaning Examples
-ate To cause or make exaggerate, activate, dominate, separate, cultivate
-en To become or make frighten, strengthen, darken, harden, sharpen
-er To perform an action repeatedly chatter, mutter, murmur, flutter, shudder
-ish To do or make something happen publish, furnish, establish, punish, banish
-ise (ize) To bring into a condition memorise (memorize), organise (organize), criticise (criticize), realise (realize), advertise
-se To perform an action cleanse, rinse, tense, offense, immerse
-fy To make or turn into justify, purify, simplify, magnify, clarify
-it To act upon audit, merit, limit, inherit, visit
