  1. Above (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates a higher position without direct contact.
    Example: The picture is above the sofa.
    Translation: ছবিটা সোফার উপরে।

  2. Across (একপাশ থেকে আরেক পাশে - Ekopash theke arek pashe):
    Indicates movement from one side to another.
    Example: She walked across the street.
    Translation: সে রাস্তার একপাশ থেকে আরেক পাশে হাঁটলো।

  3. After (পরে - Pore):
    Indicates following in time or order.
    Example: We’ll go out after dinner.
    Translation: আমরা ডিনারের পরে বাইরে যাবো।

  4. Against (বিপক্ষে - Bipokkhé):
    Indicates opposition or contact with a surface.
    Example: The ladder is leaning against the wall.
    Translation: মইটা দেওয়ালের বিপক্ষে ঠেকানো।

  5. Along (বরাবর - Borabor):
    Indicates following the length of something.
    Example: We walked along the river.
    Translation: আমরা নদীর বরাবর হাঁটলাম।

  6. Among (মধ্যে - Modhye):
    Used to indicate being part of a group.
    Example: She was among friends.
    Translation: সে বন্ধুদের মধ্যে ছিলো।

  7. Around (চারপাশে - Charpashe):
    Indicates surrounding something.
    Example: He looked around the room.
    Translation: সে ঘরের চারপাশে তাকালো।

  8. At (এ - E, কাছে - Kache):
    Used to indicate location, time, or direction.
    Example: We’ll meet at 5 PM.
    Translation: আমরা বিকেল ৫টায় দেখা করবো।

  9. Before (আগে - Agé):
    Indicates earlier in time or position.
    Example: He arrived before me.
    Translation: সে আমার আগে পৌঁছেছে।

  10. Behind (পিছনে - Pichhone):
    Indicates a position further back.
    Example: The dog is behind the tree.
    Translation: কুকুরটা গাছের পিছনে।

  11. Below (নিচে - Niché):
    Indicates a lower position.
    Example: The temperature is below zero.
    Translation: তাপমাত্রা শূন্যের নিচে।

  12. Beneath (নিচে - Niché):
    Similar to "below," often used more formally.
    Example: The floor is beneath the carpet.
    Translation: মেঝেটা কার্পেটের নিচে।

  13. Beside (পাশে - Pashe):
    Indicates next to something.
    Example: He sat beside me.
    Translation: সে আমার পাশে বসলো।

  14. Between (মধ্যে - Modhye):
    Indicates something in the middle of two or more things.
    Example: The book is between the two boxes.
    Translation: বইটা দুই বাক্সের মধ্যে।

  15. Beyond (বাইরে - Baire):
    Indicates something further than a certain point.
    Example: The village is beyond the hills.
    Translation: গ্রামটা পাহাড়ের বাইরে।

  16. But (কিন্তু - Kintu):
    Often used as a conjunction; here, it means "except."
    Example: Everyone but John attended.
    Translation: জন ছাড়া সবাই এসেছিল।

  17. By (দ্বারা - Dhyara, পাশে - Pashe):
    Indicates the agent or proximity.
    Example: The letter was written by her.
    Translation: চিঠিটা তার দ্বারা লেখা হয়েছিল।

  18. Down (নিচে - Niché):
    Indicates movement to a lower position.
    Example: He walked down the stairs.
    Translation: সে সিঁড়ি দিয়ে নিচে নামলো।

  19. During (সময়কালে - Shomoykalé):
    Indicates a period of time.
    Example: We stayed inside during the storm.
    Translation: ঝড়ের সময়কালে আমরা ভেতরে ছিলাম।

  20. For (জন্য - Jonyo):
    Indicates purpose or a period of time.
    Example: This gift is for you.
    Translation: এই উপহারটা তোমার জন্য।

  21. From (থেকে - Theke):
    Indicates the origin or starting point.
    Example: She comes from a small town.
    Translation: সে একটি ছোট শহর থেকে এসেছে।

  22. In (মধ্যে - Modhye):
    Indicates being inside a space or time period.
    Example: She lives in Dhaka.
    Translation: সে ঢাকায় থাকে।

  23. Inside (ভিতরে - Bhitore):
    Indicates being within a space.
    Example: The keys are inside the drawer.
    Translation: চাবিগুলো ড্রয়ারের ভিতরে।

  24. Into (মধ্যে - Modhye):
    Indicates movement toward the interior.
    Example: He jumped into the pool.
    Translation: সে পুলের মধ্যে লাফ দিলো।

  25. Near (কাছে - Kache):
    Indicates proximity.
    Example: The school is near the park.
    Translation: স্কুলটা পার্কের কাছে।

  26. Of (এর - Er):
    Indicates belonging or association.
    Example: A book of stories.
    Translation: গল্পের বই।

  27. Off (থেকে - Theke):
    Indicates separation or removal.
    Example: The cat jumped off the table.
    Translation: বিড়ালটা টেবিল থেকে লাফিয়ে নামলো।

  28. On (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates being in contact with a surface.
    Example: The book is on the table.
    Translation: বইটা টেবিলের উপরে।

  29. Onto (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates movement to a surface.
    Example: The cat jumped onto the roof.
    Translation: বিড়ালটা ছাদের উপরে লাফ দিলো।

  30. Out (বাইরে - Baire):
    Indicates movement away from a space.
    Example: She went out of the room.
    Translation: সে ঘর থেকে বাইরে গেলো।

  31. Outside (বাইরে - Baire):
    Indicates being external to something.
    Example: The kids are playing outside.
    Translation: বাচ্চারা বাইরে খেলছে।

  32. Over (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates above and covering.
    Example: The clouds are over the city.
    Translation: মেঘগুলো শহরের উপরে।

  33. Since (থেকে - Theke):
    Indicates time from a starting point.
    Example: I haven’t seen her since Monday.
    Translation: সোমবার থেকে তাকে দেখিনি।

  34. Through (মাধ্যমে - Madhyome):
    Indicates moving in one side and out the other.
    Example: The train went through the tunnel.
    Translation: ট্রেনটা সুড়ঙ্গের মাধ্যমে গেলো।

  35. To (প্রতি - Proti):
    Indicates direction or recipient.
    Example: She went to school.
    Translation: সে স্কুলে গেলো।

  36. Toward(s) (দিকে - Diké):
    Indicates movement in a particular direction.
    Example: He walked towards the door.
    Translation: সে দরজার দিকে হাঁটলো।

  37. Under (নিচে - Niché):
    Indicates directly below something.
    Example: The cat is under the table.
    Translation: বিড়ালটা টেবিলের নিচে।

  38. Until (পর্যন্ত - Porjonto):
    Indicates up to a certain time.
    Example: We waited until sunset.
    Translation: আমরা সূর্যাস্ত পর্যন্ত অপেক্ষা করলাম।

  39. Up (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates movement to a higher position.
    Example: She climbed up the hill.
    Translation: সে পাহাড়ের উপরে উঠলো।

  40. Upon (উপরে - Upore):
    Indicates formal or poetic usage for "on."
    Example: Once upon a time...
    Translation: অনেক অনেক দিন আগে...

  41. With (সঙ্গে - Shongé):
    Indicates accompaniment or possession.
    Example: She is with her friends.
    Translation: সে তার বন্ধুদের সঙ্গে আছে।

  42. Within (মধ্যে - Modhye):
    Indicates inside a space or time limit.
    Example: Complete it within an hour.
    Translation: এক ঘণ্টার মধ্যে শেষ করো

Prepositions for Time: A Quick Guide

  1. At
    Used for specific points in time:

    • Examples:

      • At 5 PM: We will meet at 5 PM.

      • At sunrise: The event starts at sunrise.

  2. In
    Used for longer periods such as months, years, centuries, and parts of the day:

    • Examples:

      • In August: She was born in August.

      • In 2024: The project will start in 2024.

      • In the morning: We go jogging in the morning.

  3. On
    Used for specific days and dates:

    • Examples:

      • On Monday: We have a meeting on Monday.

      • On April 1st: The new policy starts on April 1st.

      • On my birthday: Let’s celebrate on my birthday.

  4. By
    Indicates a deadline or before a certain time:

    • Examples:

      • By 10 PM: Submit your assignment by 10 PM.

      • By tomorrow: Finish the report by tomorrow.

  5. For
    Indicates the duration of time:

    • Examples:

      • For two hours: I studied for two hours.

      • For a week: They stayed here for a week.

  6. Since
    Refers to the starting point of time until now:

    • Examples:

      • Since 2015: I’ve lived here since 2015.

      • Since morning: It’s been raining since morning.

  7. During
    Used to specify when something happens within a period:

    • Examples:

      • During the meeting: Please stay quiet during the meeting.

      • During the summer: We travel during the summer.

  8. From...to/until
    Indicates the starting and ending points of time:

    • Examples:

      • From 9 AM to 5 PM: The office is open from 9 AM to 5 PM.

      • From Monday until Friday: The conference runs from Monday until Friday.

  9. Ago
    Refers to a point in the past from now:

    • Examples:

      • Two years ago: She moved here two years ago.

      • A few minutes ago: The meeting started a few minutes ago.

repositions for Place: A Quick Guide

  1. At Used for specific points or locations:

    • Examples:

      • At the bus stop: I will meet you at the bus stop.

      • At the door: She’s standing at the door.

  2. In Used for enclosed spaces or areas:

    • Examples:

      • In the room: He is in the room.

      • In the park: They are playing in the park.

  3. On Used for surfaces or specific locations within a larger area:

    • Examples:

      • On the table: The book is on the table.

      • On the street: They live on Elm Street.

  4. By Indicates proximity or nearby location:

    • Examples:

      • By the lake: The house is by the lake.

      • By the window: She is sitting by the window.

  5. For Indicates purpose or intended location:

    • Examples:

      • For the party: I bought decorations for the party.

      • For the office: The new desk is for the office.

  6. Under Indicates something beneath another object:

    • Examples:

      • Under the bed: The cat is under the bed.

      • Under the bridge: The boat passed under the bridge.

  7. Over Indicates something higher than another object:

    • Examples:

      • Over the river: The bridge goes over the river.

      • Over the table: The lamp hangs over the table.

  8. Between Indicates the location in the middle of two points:

    • Examples:

      • Between the chairs: The book is between the chairs.

      • Between New York and Boston: The town is located between New York and Boston.

  9. Behind Indicates something at the back of another object:

    • Examples:

      • Behind the building: The parking lot is behind the building.

      • Behind the curtain: She is hiding behind the curtain.

  10. Next to Indicates something immediately beside another object:

    • Examples:

      • Next to the couch: The lamp is next to the couch.

      • Next to the store: The café is next to the store.

  11. In front of Indicates something directly facing another object:

    • Examples:

      • In front of the house: The car is parked in front of the house.

      • In front of the line: He is standing in front of the line.

  12. Above Indicates something at a higher position than another object:

    • Examples:

      • Above the clouds: The plane flies above the clouds.

      • Above the desk: The painting is above the desk.

  13. Below Indicates something at a lower position than another object:

    • Examples:

      • Below the shelf: The box is below the shelf.

      • Below the mountain: The village is below the mountain.
