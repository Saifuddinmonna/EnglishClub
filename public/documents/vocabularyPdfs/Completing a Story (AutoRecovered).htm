① A Greedy Farmer

Once upon a time, there lived a poor farmer in a small village. He worked hard, but barely made enough to live. One morning, he went to his poultry yard and found a wonderful goose. This goose laid a shiny, golden egg every single day. The farmer was overjoyed. He sold the golden eggs in the market and soon became very rich. However, as he got richer, he also became greedier. He thought, 'Why wait for one egg a day? The goose must have many golden eggs inside its belly. I will get them all at once!' Driven by this foolish thought, he took a sharp knife and cut open the goose. To his great shock and dismay, he found no eggs inside. The goose was just an ordinary bird. The foolish farmer lost his daily source of gold and regretted his greedy action deeply.

② An Old Farmer and His Sons

An old farmer lived in a village with his three sons. His sons were strong and hardworking, but they always quarreled among themselves. The farmer tried many times to unite them, but he failed. As he grew older and felt his death was near, he worried about his sons' future. He called his sons to his bedside. He gave them a bundle of sticks and asked them to break it. Each son tried his best, using all his strength, but none could break the bundle. Then, the old farmer untied the bundle and gave them one stick each. He asked them to break the individual sticks. This time, they broke the sticks easily. The farmer then said, "My sons, remember this lesson. If you stay united like the bundle, no one can harm you. But if you quarrel and separate, you will be easily broken like single sticks." The sons understood their father's wisdom and promised to live in unity.

③ A Liar Shepherd

There was a young shepherd boy who looked after his village's sheep on a nearby hill. He often felt bored. One day, to amuse himself, he cried out, "Wolf! Wolf! The wolf is chasing the sheep!" The villagers heard his cries and rushed up the hill with sticks and stones to help him. When they arrived, they found no wolf. The boy laughed at them for being fooled. A few days later, the boy played the same trick again. He shouted, "Wolf! Wolf!" and again the villagers rushed to help, only to be laughed at. Then, one evening, a real wolf came into the field and started attacking the sheep. The shepherd boy cried, "Wolf! Wolf! Help me!" But this time, the villagers thought he was lying again and did not come. The wolf killed many of his sheep. The boy learned that nobody believes a liar, even when he tells the truth.

④ Grapes are Sour

One hot summer day, a fox was walking through a forest. He was very thirsty and hungry. After walking for a long time, he came to a vineyard. High up on a vine, he saw bunches of ripe, juicy grapes. His mouth watered at the sight of them. The fox jumped to reach the grapes, but they were too high. He jumped again and again, with all his might, but he could not reach them. He tried running and then leaping, but still failed. After many attempts, he was tired and frustrated. Finally, giving up, he walked away, muttering to himself, "The grapes are probably sour anyway. I wouldn't eat them even if I could reach them!" It is easy to despise what you cannot get.

⑤ Grasp All, Lose All

There was a dog who was very fond of meat. One day, he stole a large piece of meat from a butcher's shop and ran off with it. He wanted to eat it in a quiet place. On his way, he had to cross a small wooden bridge over a clear stream. As he was crossing, he looked down and saw his own reflection in the water. He thought it was another dog with an even bigger piece of meat. Being greedy, he wanted that piece too. He barked at the "other dog" to snatch its meat. As soon as he opened his mouth to bark, the piece of meat he was carrying fell into the stream and sank to the bottom. The foolish dog lost his own meat because he tried to grasp the meat he saw in the reflection. He went away hungry and sad, learning that if you try to get too much, you might lose everything.

⑥ A Thirsty Crow

It was a hot summer day. A crow became very thirsty. He flew here and there in search of water, but he could not find any. At last, he saw a pitcher under a tree. He flew down to it with hope. There was some water at the bottom of the pitcher, but it was too low for his beak to reach. The crow thought for a while. He looked around and saw some pebbles lying nearby. An idea struck him. He picked up the pebbles one by one with his beak and dropped them into the pitcher. Slowly, the water level rose. Soon, the water came up high enough for him to drink. He drank the water to his heart's content and flew away happily. This story teaches us that where there is a will, there is a way.

বাংলা অনুবাদ (Bengali Translation):

এটি ছিল এক গ্রীষ্মের গরম দিন। একটি কাক খুব তৃষ্ণার্ত হয়ে পড়ল। সে জলের খোঁজে এখানে-ওখানে উড়ে বেড়াল, কিন্তু কোথাও জল পেল না। অবশেষে, সে একটি গাছের নীচে একটি কলসি দেখতে পেল। সে আশায় বুক বেঁধে সেটির দিকে উড়ে গেল। কলসির নীচে কিছুটা জল ছিল, কিন্তু তা এত নীচে ছিল যে তার ঠোঁট পৌঁছাতে পারছিল না। কাকটি কিছুক্ষণ ভাবল। সে চারপাশে তাকাল এবং কাছে কিছু নুড়িপাথর পড়ে থাকতে দেখল। তার মাথায় একটি বুদ্ধি এল। সে তার ঠোঁট দিয়ে এক এক করে নুড়িপাথরগুলো তুলে কলসির মধ্যে ফেলল। ধীরে ধীরে, জলের স্তর উপরে উঠে এল। শীঘ্রই, জল এত উঁচুতে উঠে এল যে সে পান করতে পারল। সে তৃপ্তি সহকারে জল পান করল এবং আনন্দের সাথে উড়ে গেল। এই গল্প আমাদের শেখায় যে ইচ্ছা থাকলে উপায় হয়।
